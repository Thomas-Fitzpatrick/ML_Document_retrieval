Fundamentals of Programming and Problem Solving. (3) (MPT) Algorithm development and refinement in problem solving. Modular programming using sequence, selection, and repetition control structures. Program debugging and testing. Formatted input/output. Data files. Fundamental data types. User-defined data types: structured and enumerated. Arrays and arrays of structures. Simple sorting and searching algorithms. Character data and string processing. Algorithm efficiency considerations. Classes, objects, and introduction to object-oriented programming.2 Lec. 1 Lab.Prerequisite: MTH 102 or higher, or a score of 12 or higher on the mathematics placement test, or permission of instructor.
